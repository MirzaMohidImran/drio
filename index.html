What types of websites and stores does Drio Web build?+
We build custom eCommerce stores, product landing pages, and conversion-focused websites on Shopify, WooCommerce, Webflow, WordPress, Framer, and custom code. Every build is tailored from scratch — no shortcuts.
How much does a custom eCommerce store cost?+
A focused product landing page starts around $1,500. A full custom eCommerce store typically runs $5,000–$20,000+ depending on complexity, integrations, and catalog size. You get a fixed-price proposal after your discovery call — no hourly billing, no surprises mid-project.
How long does it take to build an eCommerce store?+
A product landing page or single-product store takes 1–2 weeks. A full multi-product eCommerce store typically takes 4–6 weeks. We give you a concrete timeline before the project starts and don't move the goalposts.
What platform should my eCommerce store be built on?+
We recommend based on your specific situation, not on what's easiest for us. Shopify for straightforward product-focused stores, WooCommerce for content-heavy or highly customized needs, Webflow for design-forward brands, and custom code for anything that needs complete flexibility. We'll tell you which one fits during your discovery call.
What kind of conversion results can I expect?+
Our clients have seen anywhere from 20% to 55% increases in conversion rates after a redesign or new build, depending on the starting point. We build with conversion as the primary goal — not just aesthetics.
Do you work with new eCommerce brands or only established stores?+
Both. We work with brands launching their first store and with established stores doing significant revenue that need a performance upgrade. The approach is different for each — we scope accordingly.
Do you offer support after launch?+
Every project includes 30 days of post-launch support. Monthly retainers are available after that for ongoing updates, CRO testing, new feature development, and performance monitoring.
How do I get started?+
Book a free 30-minute strategy call. We'll look at your current store or brief, ask the right questions, and come back with an honest roadmap and clear pricing — no pressure, no generic pitch.
